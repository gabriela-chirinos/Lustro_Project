# Lustro — Product Context

## Register
brand

## Product Purpose
Single-page marketing site for a bespoke shoe care and leather restoration service in Los Angeles. The site's job is one conversion: a booking. Not information browsing — appointment capture.

## Target Users
High-income men in greater Los Angeles (West Hollywood, Beverly Hills, Silver Lake, Marina del Rey). They own $300–$1,000+ leather shoes and boots — Alden, Allen Edmonds, Crockett & Jones, Saint Laurent, Berluti. They spend on quality without having to think about it. They dislike friction and distrust anything that reads as fast-food service. Pickup and delivery by appointment only means they never have to go anywhere.

## Brand Personality
Measured. Unhurried. Confident without announcement. Old world craft expressed in contemporary restraint. The brand doesn't sell — it presents. The word "luxury" never appears. No hyperbole, no urgency theater, no countdown timers. The feeling is: a craftsman who doesn't need to prove anything.

Voice: short declarative sentences. Present tense. No filler. No em dashes.

## Anti-References
- Any fast-fashion or sneaker-cleaning service aesthetic (bright colors, streetwear energy)
- "Premium" SaaS design patterns (gradient CTAs, card grids, generic sans)
- Overdesigned luxury (baroque ornamentation, heavy gold-on-black)
- Generic editorial minimalism (white space alone is not a voice)

## Strategic Principles
1. One conversion path. Every section exists to move toward booking. Nothing else.
2. Carlos is the brand. A 37-year craftsman who works alone. His biography is the proof.
3. Scarcity is real. By appointment, one craftsman, fewer clients per week. Design reflects this.
4. The deposit ($15) is disclosed at every CTA. No surprises = trust.
5. Photography is the gap. The site currently has no photography of the actual work. It is the biggest open risk.
